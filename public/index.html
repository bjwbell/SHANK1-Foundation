About the gene

What is the SHANK1 gene?

SHANK1 is a human gene on chromosome 19 that encodes a synaptic scaffold protein. SHANK proteins help organize molecular complexes at excitatory neuronal synapses, especially within the postsynaptic density.

Published reports and clinical gene curation efforts have linked SHANK1 deletions and truncating variants with autism spectrum disorder and broader neurodevelopmental features. Because the condition is rare, families and researchers need shared infrastructure to understand the full clinical range.

Features reported in SHANK1-related conditions may include

  • Autism spectrum disorder or autistic traits
  • Developmental delay or learning differences
  • Speech and language delay
  • Attention, behavior, or anxiety symptoms
  • Motor, coordination, or adaptive-function concerns
  • Variable expression across affected individuals and families

This site is educational and is not medical advice. Families should review genetic results with a qualified geneticist or genetic counselor.

Our mission

Improve quality of life for people affected by SHANK1-related neurodevelopmental disorders by building community, accelerating research, and supporting the path toward meaningful treatments.

The problem

  • SHANK1-related conditions are rare and under-recognized.
  • Clinical information is fragmented across families, clinics, and research studies.
  • There is limited disease-specific guidance for families after diagnosis.
  • Research progress depends on organized data and engaged participants.
